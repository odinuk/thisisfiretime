Questions? Answers.

How do I create a quick workout?
From the home screen, tap the plus icon. Choose your workout duration, style (HIIT, EMOM, Tabata, etc.), and focus area (full body, upper, lower, core). FireTime will generate a complete workout with exercises — ready in about 10 seconds.
How do I use Weights mode?
Weights mode is designed for gym sessions. Tap the plus icon and select "Weights" as your structure. Pick your exercises (up to 8), and set your rest time between exercises (60s, 90s, or 120s). Each exercise is an open interval — work at your own pace and tap Done when you're finished. The app tracks your sets for you. The app will give you a timed rest before the next exercise.
What is an open interval?
An open interval has no countdown — you work at your own pace and tap Done when you're ready to move on. This is useful for strength training where you want to control your tempo, or any exercise where a fixed timer doesn't fit. You can add open intervals to any custom workout by setting the duration to 0 in the workout builder.
Can I create my own custom workouts?
Yes! Tap "New Workout" to open the workout builder. You can add intervals, set work and rest times, name your exercises, and save your workout for future use. Your custom workouts are stored locally on your device.
How do I pause a workout?
Tap the pause button during any workout to pause the timer. Tap again to resume. On Apple Watch, swipe right to access pause and end controls.
How do I edit or delete a saved workout?
Tap on any saved workout to open it in the workout builder, where you can edit intervals, durations, and names. To delete a workout, swipe left on the workout card from the home screen and tap Delete.
How do I reorder my workouts?
Long-press on any workout card and drag it to your preferred position. Your custom order is saved automatically.
Does FireTime work with Apple Watch?
Yes! FireTime includes a companion Apple Watch app. Start a workout on your iPhone and it syncs to your watch, or start directly from your watch. You'll see the timer, current exercise, and upcoming intervals right on your wrist.
How does Apple Health integration work?
When you complete a workout, FireTime can log it to Apple Health (with your permission). This includes workout duration, estimated calories burned, and heart rate data if you're wearing an Apple Watch. You can enable or disable this in Settings.

Troubleshooting

Why don't I see calories or heart rate in Apple Fitness?
If you have an Apple Watch, FireTime uses your Watch's sensors for accurate heart rate and calorie data. For this to work, you need to grant Health permissions on both your iPhone and your Watch. On your Watch, go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → FireTime and make sure Active Calories and Heart Rate are enabled. Without an Apple Watch, FireTime saves workouts with estimated calories (based on duration) but no heart rate data.
Do I need to create an account?
No. FireTime works completely offline and doesn't require any account, login, or personal information. Just download and start training.
Can I use FireTime without an Apple Watch?
Absolutely. The Apple Watch app is optional. FireTime works perfectly on iPhone alone with audio cues and haptic feedback to guide you through your workout.
My workouts disappeared. How do I get them back?
Workouts are stored locally on your device. If you deleted and reinstalled the app, or got a new device, the workouts won't transfer automatically. We recommend taking screenshots of important custom workouts as a backup. A cloud sync feature may be added in a future update.
